MAKING MONEY IN A BEAR MARKET

Bearcoin is the first decentralized financial instrument to allow investors to profit when the price of Bitcoin falls. When Bitcoin falls, Bearcoin is designed to rise proportionally in value.

PROBLEMS WITH SHORT SELLING

For a long time wealthy and experienced investors have used short selling to turn a profit during a market downturn. In a short sale, investors sell borrowed assets that they hope to purchase later at a lower price in order to repay the lender.

This is an incredibly risky proposition since your losses are theoretically unlimited if the price of the asset moves up instead of down. Even in traditional markets, short selling is considered too risky for most investors. The high volatility of crypto markets makes short selling all the more lethal.

Additionally, short selling is not decentralized or trustless. Investors must trust the exchange - a centralized entity - to perform its duties without error, fraud, or service outage.

PROBLEMS WITH OTHER TOKENS

Negatively correlated tokens already exist. However, there is a cost. Ultimately these tokens are still linked to traditional financial instruments (such as short selling) on exchanges, which means they are not decentralized and they can be expensive. Traditional financial instruments use “margin”, which is borrowed money that must be repaid with interest. This puts a slow drain on the overall value of the token. Additionally, these tokens may charge high management fees - as much as 0.03% per day.

ENGINEERED INFLATION AND DEFLATION

Bearcoin is an immutable smart contract existing on the Polygon blockchain (which is an Ethereum-compatible blockchain but with MUCH lower transaction fees than Ethereum). The Bearcoin contract uses Chainlink’s BTC/USD price feed as a decentralized price oracle to determine the price movement of Bitcoin.

Using this real-time information about price fluctuations in Bitcoin, Bearcoin decides whether to inflate or deflate itself each time Bearcoin tokens change hands. This inflation or deflation is capped at a max of 5% of the transaction’s value.

If Bitcoin is rising in value, Bearcoin inflates itself by up to 5% per transaction, and these newly minted Bearcoins are paid out to a randomly selected address in the Inflation Pool (more on that later).

Conversely, when the price of Bitcoin falls, a transaction fee is burned on each transfer, deflating the currency.

Since the maximum rate of engineered inflation/deflation depends on Bearcoin volume, there may be a lag behind the movement of Bitcoin until enough BTCBEAR changes hands to allow the inflation/deflation to catch up to the correct level.

For example, if Bitcoin drops 20% in a day and then stays level, Bearcoin will use whatever trading volume is available to eventually deflate the currency by 20%, theoretically driving up the value of each Bearcoin by 20%.

The fact that Bearcoin uses engineered inflation and deflation rather than a “fiat” mechanism like rebasing is what leads to its interesting tokenomics - inflation mining, exchange-friendly swaps, etc.

A NOVEL INFLATION MECHANISM: INFLATION MINING

Bearcoin rewards buyers during inflationary periods by distributing the inflation generated during each transaction to a randomly selected address in the Inflation Pool. Any user who holds Bearcoin can visit the bearcoin.io dApp, connect to Metamask, and enable inflation mining on their account.

After inflation mining is enabled, as long as that account maintains a balance of at least 100 BTCBEAR, it has a chance of receiving the inflation generated from every transaction. Inflation is distributed by drawing 10 random accounts from the inflation pool, and awarding inflation to the one with the highest balance.

In this way, Bearcoin allows anyone to “mine inflation” by simply enabling inflation mining and then holding at least 100 BTCBEAR in their account (and the higher a balance they maintain, the better odds they have of receiving inflation).

For example, if a whale happens to buy $100K of Bearcoin during an inflationary period at the maximum inflation rate of 5%, the inflation generated ($5K of Bearcoin) could be minted to an inflation miner who had a balance of only 110 BTCBEAR. This is possible (though unlikely) because it’s possible the other 9 randomly selected accounts had balances of 100-109 BTCBEAR.

One tenth of one percent (0.01%) of inflation goes toward hosting fees for Bearcoin.io, updating the Chainlink price feed, etc. In addition to allowing users to enable inflation mining, Bearcoin.io provides basic information and useful trading statistics: current total supply, current rate of inflation/deflation, etc.

A NOVEL DEFLATION MECHANISM: DEX-FRIENDLY DEFLATION

Deflationary tokens do not normally play well with decentralized exchanges like Uniswap (they have a bad habit of breaking Liquidity Pool contracts, allowing attackers to steal millions of dollars, etc). So Bearcoin uses a two-pronged approach to deflation: first, during a deflationary cycle (when Bitcoin is going down), Bearcoin marks up to 5% of each transaction as deflation, but initially records the full transaction amount. This makes exchanges happy, since the full value was received, and the swap succeeds.

The deflation address and amount are recorded in the contract, and the very next transaction (most likely involving a completely different user) will complete the deflation. So the user would see two transactions probably very close together - the initial transfer of the full amount, and then a second transaction burning the deflation portion. A clever user might try to quickly withdraw the portion marked for deflation before it was burned, but this is prevented by the smart contract.

The second mechanism Bearcoin uses to be DEX-friendly is to only deflate accounts that have opted into inflation mining: any account that gets the benefit of inflation mining is also subject to deflation. Since the smart contracts that make up exchanges like Uniswap will never opt into inflation mining, exchange accounts will also never be subject to deflation, and so will have predictable balances and can treat BTCBEAR just like any other ERC20 token.

RISKS AND CHALLENGES

Let us be clear: Bearcoin is highly experimental. Despite its numerous advantages over traditional financial instruments, we cannot definitively predict the price action of Bearcoin in relation to Bitcoin. Inflation and deflation will exert predictable economic pressures upon the price of Bearcoin, but ultimately the price will be determined by a combination of factors, including overall market demand for the token itself.

Additionally, we do not know what the initial price of Bearcoin should be. Since we cannot possibly predict this, our objective is to launch Bearcoin in an equitable manner and trust that it will find its correct price over time.

INITIAL TOKEN DISTRIBUTION

To achieve an equitable distribution with an unknown market cap, the initial token distribution will heavily prioritize free airdrops (38%), and the initial token sale will be conservatively priced (0.10 USDC = 1 BTCBEAR) and go entirely toward funding the liquidity pool (30% sold for USDC, combined with 30% of the Bearcoin supply = 60% to the liquidity pool). The resulting Liquidity Pool tokens will be locked for 5 years in order to guarantee the pool remains stable (after 5 years, max withdrawal of 5% per year).

AIRDROP MECHANICS

To encourage holding and interest in BTCBEAR during the critical early adoption phase, starting 30 days after the contract is deployed an airdrop will automatically run for 12 months and distribute each day's amount (38% of total supply / 365 days = ~21,868 BTCBEAR per day) to random addresses in the inflation pool (all accounts with inflation mining enabled and holding at least 100 BTCBEAR). Each distribution will be a random portion of that day's airdrop amount, so it's likely that both very large and very small distribtions will happen on the same day. Each airdrop will be a minimum of 100 BTCBEAR (except for the very last one of each day, which will distribute the remainder for the day even if it's below 100 BTCBEAR). Unlike inflation distributions, the balance of each account is not considered by the airdrop: so every account in the inflation pool has the same odds of receiving an airdrop.

ONLY THE BEGINNING

Bearcoin (BTCBEAR) is linked to the fate of Bitcoin, but in future it would be trivial to tweak and redeploy the contract, thus launching related tokens which target other markets - perhaps targeting downtrends in ETH or BNB.

NOT A SECURITY

Because Bearcoin is a purely algorithmic system without direct human involvement or control, and moves in relation to Bitcoin (which according to SEC Chair Jay Clayton is not a security), our belief is that Bearcoin is not a security. At the very least, we believe Bearcoin definitely does NOT meet part 4 of the Howey Test and so it should not be classified as a security. Therefore, it should be tradable on US-based exchanges.

CONCLUSION

Bearcoin provides a novel contrarian investment vehicle targeted initially at Bitcoin, but with massive potential to expand and launch other contrarian tokens on the same infrastructure.

It provides theoretical risk levels similar to long investments, coupled to the power and utility of short investments, with a novel “inflation mining” mechanism to support trading volume and a novel “DEX-friendly deflation” mechanism to allow easy trading.
